Ireland

Ireland is a sovereign state in Europe that occupies about five-sixths of the island of the same name. It is a constitutional republic governed as a parliamentary democracy, with a president elected to serve as head of state. Considered a developed country, Ireland has the third highest Human Development Index (HDI) in the world, in addition to excellent rankings in indices that measure the degree of democracy and freedoms such as press, economic and political freedom.

Ireland has two official languages, Irish and English. Even though only 2% of the population has Irish as their main language (this small portion of individuals is located in Galway), schools have a compulsory language teaching and most people in the country have a basic understanding of it. Furthermore, the National Anthem of Ireland is entirely in Irish.

Ireland is a country that does not stand out in the gastronomic world. Well, cooking has a certain simplicity both in the preparation, in the amount of ingredients, and in the final dish. The main ingredient of this cuisine is the potato, which is in most Irish dishes. The great love for potatoes comes from "great hunger ", which took place in 1840. Some typical Irish dishes are: Irish Breakfast, Dublin Coddle, Irish Stew, Shepherd's Pie, Fish and Chips.

As in much of Europe, the currency in Ireland is the euro. In Ireland, most purchases are made using a debit card rather than a credit card and there is rarely an option to pay in installments. Another interesting fact is that only coins are accepted on buses.

Dublin

Period: 3 days

It is the capital and largest city of Ireland. The English name derives from the Irish word "Dubhlinn", which means "Black Lake". Located in province of Leinster near the midpoint of the east coast of Ireland, being bisected by the River Liffey and the center of the Dublin region. Since 1898 it has been county administrative level. Its boundaries are the counties of Fingal to the north, southern Dublin to the south-west and Dun Laoghaire-Rathdown to the south-east. It has a population of 527,612 inhabitants in the city, and its metropolitan area has 1,809,156 inhabitants. Dublin is recognized as a global city, placing the city among the 30 most globalized in the world. It is currently the main historical, cultural, economic, industrial and educational center of Ireland.

Galway

Period: 2 days

Distance from Dublin: 186.0 km

It is the county town of County Galway, which is named after the county. River Corrib between Lough Corrib and Galway Bay, and is the sixth most populous city on the island of Ireland, with a 2022 census population of 83,456. Located near a previous settlement, Galway grew around a fort built by the King of Connacht in 1124. A municipal charter, received in 1484, allowed the citizens of the then walled city to form a council and town hall for the city. Controlled largely by a group of merchant families, the Tribes of Galway. The city grew and became a trading port. After a period of decline, starting in the 21st century, the city is a tourist destination and is known for hosting numerous festivals, celebrations and events, including the Galway Arts Festival.

Cork

Period: 2 days

Distance from Galway: 157.4 km

Distance from Dublin: 220.6 km

Cork is the second largest city in the Republic of Ireland and the third most populous on the island of Ireland (the capital Dublin being the most populous). It is the capital and principal city of County Cork and also the largest city in Munster. Since 1898 the city has had administrative county status (county-boroughs). Cork is the heart of the industry in the south of Ireland. Its main industry is pharmaceuticals, with Pfizer Inc and the Novartis, which ensure many jobs for the region. The most famous product of the pharmaceutical industry in Cork is Viagra.
