In Dublin

St. Patrick's Cathedral

St Patrick's Cathedral is that place you should know in your first few hours in Dublin. It is an exuberant medieval church, similar to those found throughout Ireland. It is the largest in the country and was built between 1254 and 1270. Always remembering that Saint Patrick is the patron saint of Ireland. The church has a beautiful garden where people usually sit on the benches, admiring the local landscape. It is located on Dublin 8, a 10-minute walk from the center.

Guiness Storehouse

Guinness beer is Ireland's greatest heritage. Even if you don't drink beer, you should at least taste a pint of Guinness at a local pub. At the Guinness Storehouse you will learn how the drink is made and taste it straight from the source at Gravity Bar, a round bar surrounded by glass on the seventh floor of the building, offering a panoramic view of the city.

Phoenix Park

Simply the biggest urban park in Europe. It is located 3 km from the center, on Dublin 8, and has a circumference of 16 km. It is almost impossible to see the entire park. On foot we see little. It is necessary to rent a bike to enjoy it better. So you can find the deer that roam free in Phoenix Park. cricket, football and hurling, a polo field, monuments, exhibitions art centers and even the president's official residence. Phoenix Park is open 24 hours a day, seven days a week, all year round.

St. Stephen's Green

St Stephen's Green is the coziest park in the city which is located on Dublin 2, close to Grafton Street. This street (boardwalk), with famous shops and street artists, is also a tourist spot in Dublin where you will be walking around a lot. The park has been used as a setting for movies and is a meeting point on sunny days. It is usually full in summer, and its landscapes in autumn are breathtaking. A park right in the center of a cosmopolitan city makes people use it as a path, just to feel the pleasure of being in the middle of nature.

Patrick Guilbaud

The Patrick Guilbaud restaurant, one of the most elegant places to eat in the city. It is the only holder of two Michelin stars in Ireland. The atmosphere is very elegant and the staff are very friendly. The owner, who gives the restaurant its name, is also always walking around the tables, being a very approachable person who loves to admire the atmosphere of his restaurant. Perhaps that's why the restaurant has been attracting so many customers for over 30 years! The dishes have classic French and Irish roots, but keep in mind that the dishes don't cost less than 90 euros.

Chapter One

A fantastic option for those looking for the best restaurants in Dublin is the Michelin-starred Chapter One restaurant. The location is prime as the site is beneath the Dublin Writers Museum, which is also worth a visit. In addition to the fantastic dishes, the entire environment was thought out with the utmost care and to the smallest detail, as the owners' intention is to provide a multisensory experience while customers dine. For this, they made everything around them by Irish artists: sculptures, hand-woven baskets; the table in the Jameson Room (a space for more private meals) among other details. As for the food, it can be said that they are Irish dishes but with an international touch. The lunch menu usually costs €45, while the dinner menu, which consists of four courses, costs €80.

In Galway

Galway City Museum

Galway City Museum is a museum in Galway City, County Galway, Ireland. It was founded on July 29, 2006 and is located next to the Spanish Arch.

Aniar

Aniar has the enviable award of being Galway's only Michelin-starred restaurant, having been awarded its prestigious star in September 2012. Aniar is an innovative restaurant, bringing meals based on 'terroir' to Dominick Street in Galway City. The word 'terroir' is generally associated with winemaking and the combination of factors that give a wine its distinctive character: soil, climate and environment. Aniar, owned by the people who brought you Cava Restaurant (next to Aniar) is focused on local ingredients from the land and natural influences. More Information

In Cork

Blarney Castle

One of Ireland's landmarks that preserves a history of hundreds of years is in County Cork and is also one of the most visited places in the country. Considered a treasure in Ireland, Blarney Castle itself is a landmark, but a visit to the castle is made even more interesting by the attractions it carries with it.

Blarney Castle that can be seen today is the third building, the first being built in the 10th century on a wooden structure. Around 1210 AD, it was replaced by a stone one, but later demolished. Only in 1446, the third castle, with some parts still remaining in the original shape to this day, was built by Dermot McCarthy, King of Munster. When visiting the castle, it is possible to see it from various angles to get an idea of its various “interpretations”. That's because, from certain points of view, the castle appears to be more magical. From others, more warrior and medieval; of others, taller, imposing. That is, it all depends on where you look at it. From the top of the castle, it is possible to contemplate wonderful views of more than 60 hectares of extensive parks that include gardens, avenues, shrubs and canals.
